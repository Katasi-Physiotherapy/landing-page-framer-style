## What is Dry Needling?

Simply put, dry needling is a modern therapeutic technique that uses thin, sterile needles, without medication to target and deactivate **myofascial trigger points (MTrPs)**.

These trigger points are often the culprits behind chronic muscle pain, stiffness, and reduced movement. 

Unlike traditional *acupuncture*, which is rooted in Traditional Chinese Medicine, **dry needling** is firmly based on Western anatomical and neurophysiological principles, making it a **precise** and **scientifically-backed approach** to pain relief.

## How Does Dry Needling Work? The Science Behind the Relief

At Katasi Physiotherapist, we believe in transparent and understandable treatment. The effectiveness of dry needling stems from several physiological mechanisms:

1. **Deactivating Trigger Points:** Myofascial trigger points are hyperirritable spots within a taut band of skeletal muscle. 

   When a dry needle is inserted into these points, it elicits a **Local Twitch Response (LTR).** 

   This LTR is crucial as it mechanically disrupts the dysfunctional endplates within the trigger area, leading to their resolution and immediate muscle relaxation .
2. **Biochemical Changes:** Research has shown that eliciting an LTR during dry needling leads to significant biochemical changes within the MTrPs. 

   Real-time blood micro-sampling studies have demonstrated a normalization of the chemical environment around the trigger point, reducing inflammatory markers and pain-producing substances.
3. **Neurological Pain Inhibition:** The insertion of the needle stimulates *A-delta receptors*, which are nerve fibers responsible for transmitting sharp, localized pain. 

   This stimulation triggers the release of *endogenous opioids,* the body's natural pain relievers, providing a powerful analgesic effect and inhibiting pain signals.
